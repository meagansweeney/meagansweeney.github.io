It is worth noting that book banning or censorship is not limited to these countries and occurs in various forms all over the world, often as a means of suppressing dissent or controlling access to information.

Highly Restrictive Countries

Country Flag Restrictions
China The Chinese government has a long history of banning books and restricting freedom of expression. They often ban books that are critical of the government or promote ideas that are deemed politically sensitive or threatening to the ruling Communist Party.
North Korea The North Korean government tightly controls access to information and has banned numerous books and other forms of media that are deemed critical of the regime or promote ideas that are not in line with the country's official ideology.
Saudi Arabia Saudi Arabia's strict interpretation of Islamic law has led to the banning of books that are deemed offensive to Islam, promote non-Muslim religions, or contain sexually explicit content.
Iran The Iranian government has banned numerous books over the years, including those that are critical of the government, promote secularism, or contain content deemed offensive to Islam.
Russia The Russian government has banned books that are critical of the government or promote ideas that are not in line with the country's official ideology. In recent years, there has been a growing trend of banning books that contain LGBTQ+ content.
